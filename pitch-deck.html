02 / 10 Who — Vision & Contributors 26 Feb 2026
WHO WE ARE & WHY WE CARE

The Vision

Every person who walks into a public library deserves to be understood. Language should never be a barrier to knowledge, services, or belonging.

Babel Fish was born from the daily reality at Georges River Libraries, where staff serve one of the most linguistically diverse communities in Australia. Our vision is simple yet transformative: eliminate the communication gap between library staff and non-English speaking patrons — instantly, accurately, and with dignity.

Contributors

  • Courtney Wright — Lead Developer & Project Architect
  • Georges River Libraries — Domain Expertise & User Research
  • Georges River Council — Organisational Sponsor

Guiding Principles

  • Inclusivity — Every language matters equally
  • Practicality — Built for real library workflows
  • Accessibility — WCAG 2.1 AA compliant design
  • Scalability — Designed to grow with community needs

Community Context

The Georges River Local Government Area in southern Sydney is home to speakers of over 60 languages. Mandarin, Cantonese, Nepali, Greek, Arabic, Macedonian, Spanish, Italian, and Indonesian represent the most frequently encountered languages at the library service desk. Babel Fish transforms this linguistic richness from a challenge into a strength.

03 / 10 What — The Product 26 Feb 2026
WHAT BABEL FISH DOES

A Digital Phrasebook — And More

Babel Fish is a full-stack web application that provides instant, accurate translations and audio playback of common library-related enquiries and responses. It bridges the communication gap between staff and patrons from diverse linguistic backgrounds.

Core Features

  • Multilingual Phrasebook — Essential phrases in 9+ community languages
  • Dual-User Modes — Tailored interfaces for staff and customers
  • Text-to-Speech — Native audio playback with correct pronunciation
  • AI Suggestions — Gemini-powered contextual phrase generation

Advanced Capabilities

  • Speech Recognition — Real-time voice input via Web Speech API
  • Interactive Maps — Google Maps directions to Centrelink
  • QR Code Access — Scannable codes for mobile directions
  • Firebase Sync — Real-time conversation logging

09 / 10 Why — The Problem 26 Feb 2026
THE PROBLEM OF LINGUISTIC FRAGMENTATION

The Silence at the Desk

Every day, in libraries across Australia, a patron approaches the service desk with a question they cannot ask. The staff member sees the need but cannot bridge the gap. Both parties leave the interaction diminished. This is linguistic fragmentation — not the absence of language, but the collision of languages without a shared bridge.

300+ Languages Spoken Across Australia
22% Australians Speak Non-English at Home
0 Dedicated Library Translation Tools

For Staff

  • Inability to assist patrons despite willingness
  • Reliance on ad-hoc gestures and guesswork
  • Professional frustration and service inconsistency
  • No purpose-built tools for library contexts

For Patrons

  • Exclusion from services they are entitled to
  • Anxiety and loss of dignity in public settings
  • Inability to access critical information
  • Dependence on family members as interpreters

The Gap in the Market

Generic translation apps like Google Translate exist, but they are not designed for structured service interactions. They lack curated library-specific vocabulary, dual-mode interfaces, category-based phrase organisation, and the ability to generate contextual suggestions. Libraries need a tool built for libraries.

10 / 10 Why — The Imperative 26 Feb 2026
THE CASE FOR UNIVERSAL TRANSLATION

Why This Matters — Now

Public libraries are the last truly universal civic institution. They are free, open, and serve everyone regardless of background. But "everyone" is an empty promise when a quarter of the population cannot communicate their needs at the service desk.

The Civic Argument

Libraries are gateways to settlement, education, and social inclusion. When language barriers prevent access, we fail the communities who need us most. Babel Fish is not a convenience — it is an equity tool.

The Practical Argument

Professional interpreter services are costly, require scheduling, and are impractical for brief transactional queries. Babel Fish provides instant, free, always-available translation precisely where it is needed.

The Technological Moment

Advances in AI language models, browser-native speech APIs, and cloud infrastructure make it possible — for the first time — to deliver high-quality, real-time, multilingual translation as a lightweight web application. The technology is ready. The need is urgent. The gap is ours to close.
