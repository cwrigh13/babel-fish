## STAFF SCENARIOS

### Scenario S1: Select a Display Language
**Context**: A customer who speaks Mandarin has just approached the service desk. You need to set the app to show Mandarin translations so you can communicate with them.
**Workflow Steps**:
1. Confirm the app is in "For Staff" mode (the teal button should be active). If not, tap "For Staff".
2. Find the row of language buttons below the mode selector.
3. Tap "Mandarin" to select it as the display language.
4. Scroll down and verify that each phrase card now shows English text on top with a Mandarin translation underneath.
**Success State**:
- The "Mandarin" language button is highlighted/active.
- Every phrase card displays the English phrase in bold with the Mandarin translation below it.

### Scenario S2: Play an Audio Phrase for a Customer
**Context**: A Greek-speaking customer needs help but there is a language barrier. You want to play a greeting phrase out loud so the customer can understand.
**Workflow Steps**:
1. Make sure you are in "For Staff" mode.
2. Select "Greek" from the language buttons.
3. Scroll to find a greeting phrase (e.g. "Welcome to the library" or "How can I help you?").
4. Tap the coloured speaker button on that phrase card.
5. Listen to the audio that plays and observe the button state while it is speaking.
**Success State**:
- The browser's text-to-speech reads the phrase aloud in Greek.
- The speaker button visually changes while audio is playing.
- Audio can be stopped by pressing Escape or tapping the button again.

### Scenario S3: Filter Phrases by Category
**Context**: You are helping a customer with a book loan and only want to see transaction-related phrases, not the entire list.
**Workflow Steps**:
1. In "For Staff" mode, select any language.
2. Find the category filter buttons (All, General Enquiries, Transactional, Digital Services).
3. Tap "Transactional" to filter the list.
4. Scroll through the results and confirm only borrowing/returning/renewal phrases appear.
5. Tap "All" to reset the filter and confirm all phrases reappear.
**Success State**:
- Only Transactional phrases are shown when that filter is active.
- The active filter button is visually highlighted.
- Tapping "All" restores the full phrase list.

### Scenario S4: Use the Mandarin/Cantonese Preference Card
**Context**: A Chinese-speaking customer has approached but you are not sure if they prefer Mandarin or Cantonese. The app has a special card to help you ask.
**Workflow Steps**:
1. In "For Staff" mode, select "Mandarin" as your language.
2. Scroll to the "General Enquiries" section and find the card that says "Do you prefer to speak Mandarin or Cantonese?"
3. Tap the small Chinese-text button on that card to open the language preference popup.
4. In the popup, tap one of the language options (Mandarin, Cantonese, or Either).
5. Listen to the audio confirmation that plays after your selection.
**Success State**:
- A popup appears with three language options displayed in Chinese characters.
- Tapping an option plays the corresponding audio phrase.
- The popup closes automatically after a short delay.

### Scenario S5: Help a Customer with Digital Services
**Context**: A Nepali-speaking customer needs help using the library computers and printer. You want to find the right phrases quickly.
**Workflow Steps**:
1. In "For Staff" mode, select "Nepali" as the display language.
2. Tap the "Digital Services" category filter.
3. Browse the filtered list for phrases about computers, printing, or scanning.
4. Play at least one phrase using the speaker button to confirm audio works in Nepali.
5. Switch back to "All" to confirm the full list returns.
**Success State**:
- Only Digital Services phrases are displayed when filtered.
- Phrase cards show Nepali translations alongside English.
- Audio playback works correctly for the Nepali phrases.

## CUSTOMER SCENARIOS

### Scenario C1: Switch to Customer Mode and Choose Your Language
**Context**: You are a customer who speaks Arabic. The app may currently be in Staff mode. You need to switch it to Customer mode and select your language.
**Workflow Steps**:
1. Look at the two large buttons near the top ("For Staff" and "For Customers").
2. Tap "For Customers" to switch to customer mode.
3. Notice that the language buttons now show the native language name prominently (e.g. "العربية") with the English name smaller underneath.
4. Tap "Arabic" (العربية) to select your language.
5. Scroll down and confirm that phrase cards now show the Arabic translation as the large primary text with English below it.
**Success State**:
- Customer mode is active (the red "For Customers" button is highlighted).
- Language buttons display native names prominently.
- Phrase cards show Arabic text large and bold, with English text smaller below.

### Scenario C2: Browse and Listen to General Assistance Phrases
**Context**: You are a Spanish-speaking customer who has just entered the library for the first time and needs basic help.
**Workflow Steps**:
1. Make sure you are in "For Customers" mode.
2. Select "Spanish" (Espanol) as your language.
3. Tap the "General Assistance" (Asistencia General) category filter.
4. Browse the list of available phrases in this category.
5. Tap the speaker button on any phrase to hear it spoken aloud in English.
**Success State**:
- Only General Assistance phrases are displayed.
- Category filter buttons show the native-language name prominently.
- Tapping the speaker button plays the English audio for the customer to hear.

### Scenario C3: Get Walking Directions to Centrelink
**Context**: You are at Hurstville Library and need to find the nearest Centrelink office. The app has a special interactive card for this.
**Workflow Steps**:
1. Switch to "For Customers" mode and select any language.
2. Browse the phrases or filter to find the card that says "How do I get to Centrelink?"
3. Tap on that phrase card (not the speaker button, but the card itself).
4. A popup should appear showing a Google Map with walking directions.
5. Look for the QR code at the bottom of the popup and confirm it is visible and scannable.
6. Close the popup by tapping the X button or clicking outside it.
**Success State**:
- Tapping the Centrelink card opens a directions popup.
- The popup contains an embedded Google Map showing the walking route.
- A QR code is displayed that links to Google Maps directions.
- The popup closes cleanly.

### Scenario C4: Explore Library Layout Information
**Context**: You are an Indonesian-speaking customer trying to find out where different areas of the library are located.
**Workflow Steps**:
1. In "For Customers" mode, select "Indonesian" (Bahasa Indonesia).
2. Find and tap the "Library Layout" (Tata Letak Perpustakaan) category filter.
3. Browse through the library layout phrases.
4. Check that each card shows the Indonesian translation prominently with the English version below.
5. Play one phrase to verify audio works.
**Success State**:
- Only Library Layout phrases are shown when filtered.
- Indonesian translations appear as the primary text on each card.
- The category filter button shows the Indonesian translation for "Library Layout".

### Scenario C5: Test Switching Between All Customer Categories
**Context**: You are a new Italian-speaking customer and want to explore everything the app has to offer across all categories.
**Workflow Steps**:
1. In "For Customers" mode, select "Italian" (Italiano).
2. Start with "All" filter active and note the total number of phrase cards visible.
3. Tap through each category filter one by one: General Assistance, Transactional, Library Layout, Language & Community Resources, Digital Services.
4. For each category, confirm that the displayed phrases change and are relevant to that topic.
5. Return to "All" and confirm the full list is restored.
**Success State**:
- Each of the 5 customer category filters works and shows different phrases.
- The active filter is visually highlighted.
- Returning to "All" restores the complete phrase list.
- Italian translations display correctly in every category.
